2. Ebbinghaus Illusion

How it works: A circle surrounded by small circles appears larger than the same-sized circle surrounded by large circles. This exploits size-contrast perception in human vision.

Which center circle appears largest to you?

Expected human answer: Most humans perceive option B (small surrounding circles) as having the largest center.
Machine answer: Computer vision measures all center circles as equal size.

3. Simultaneous Contrast Illusion

How it works: The same grey square appears lighter on a black background and darker on a white background. This exploits brightness adaptation in human color perception.

Which center square appears lightest to you?

Expected human answer: Most humans perceive option A (black background) as lightest.
Machine answer: Computer vision measures all center squares as the same grey value (#888888).
